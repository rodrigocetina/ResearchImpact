Document Validation Engine

The system attempts to resolve and validate every declared activity from external sources first — ORCID, CrossRef, and OpenAlex. Faculty are only asked to upload documents that are genuinely unavailable externally (internal grants, industry agreements, scanned letters). This minimises admin burden while keeping verification rigorous.

Design Principle: External-First, Upload-as-Fallback
The system attempts to resolve every declared activity from ORCID, CrossRef, and OpenAlex before requesting any upload. For a faculty member with a well-maintained ORCID profile and publications in indexed journals, the majority of their annual submission may require zero document uploads. Uploads are reserved for information that is genuinely unavailable externally — internal agreements, pending filings, proprietary contracts.

Build Sequence Recommendation
Phase 1: ORCID + CrossRef + OpenAlex integration — implement the auto-resolution gate first. This immediately eliminates upload friction for indexed publications (likely 60–70% of all submissions) with no ML required.  Phase 2: LLM grant extraction + CrossRef Funder Registry for the upload path.  Phase 3: ABS dataset import + Scimago + full three-source reconciliation engine with admin review queue.  Phase 4: Dimensions / Lens.org for patents and policy citations.  Throughout: track the "upload avoidance rate" as a KPI — the share of submissions resolved without a faculty upload — to demonstrate admin burden reduction.
⚠️
Key Constraints to Plan Around
ABS data requires an institutional licence from CABS — it cannot be scraped or accessed via public API.  ORCID completeness varies by discipline and career stage; humanities and early-career researchers often have sparse profiles.  Grant document formats from industry partners and regional government bodies vary widely — LLM extraction will need a human-in-the-loop for high-value grants above a defined threshold (e.g. >€100k).
